## OpenAI

- **Positioning:** OpenAI frames itself as "a research and deployment company" and in 2026 spun up a dedicated **"OpenAI Deployment Company"** — a $4B+ venture whose explicit job is to embed Forward Deployed Engineers into enterprises. Category claim: deployment is now as critical as model development itself.

- **Hero / key language (verbatim from announcement):**
  - "OpenAI was founded as a research and deployment company. From the beginning, we have believed that building powerful AI models is only part of the work. Real impact comes from helping people and organizations use those systems safely, effectively, and at scale."
  - "The OpenAI Deployment Company will extend OpenAI's ability to embed engineers specialized in frontier AI deployment, known as Forward Deployed Engineers, or FDEs, into organizations working on complex problems in demanding environments."
  - FDE model described: "Instead of starting with a general product, FDE teams work directly with customers to solve a specific problem, validate impact, and then identify patterns that can scale. This approach helps organizations move from AI experimentation to reliable deployment."
  - Careers (FDE role): "Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) lead complex end-to-end deployments of frontier models in production alongside our most strategic customers. They own discovery, technical scoping, system design, build, and production rollout."
  - Success metric language: "measure success through production adoption, measurable workflow impact, and eval-driven feedback that changes product and model roadmaps."

- **IA (section order):** N/A as a marketing site — split across a press/index announcement page, a `business/` deployment-company page, and individual careers JDs (by city + by vertical: Life Sciences, Semiconductor, Gov).

- **Proof pattern:** Named credibility via the venture structure itself ($4B+ initial investment; TPG-led; Bain/Capgemini/McKinsey as founding partners [unverified specifics — from secondary press]; acquired London consultancy **Tomoro**, ~150 engineers, on day one). No anonymized case studies — proof is scale + frontier-lab brand.

- **Engagement framing:** Embed → solve a specific problem → validate impact → extract repeatable patterns that scale. "End-to-end deployments... own discovery, technical scoping, system design, build, and production rollout." Strongly **build-and-into-production**; verticalized (Life Sciences, Semiconductor, Gov). Less explicit on perpetual *operation* than Ajolote's stake.

- **Visual/motion:** OpenAI house style — light, minimal, generous whitespace, monochrome with restraint. Not a reference for Ajolote's dark/cinematic direction.

- **CTA:** Careers-style ("Apply"); business page routes to enterprise/sales contact. No single consumer CTA.

- **Steal / Avoid for Ajolote:**
  - **Steal:** The phrase architecture — "research and deployment company," "embed engineers... into organizations working on complex problems in demanding environments," "move from AI experimentation to reliable deployment." This legitimizes Ajolote's whole thesis in language a frontier lab now owns. Steal the "solve one specific problem → validate impact → patterns that scale" arc (it mirrors Ajolote's FDE-library-compounds story). Steal the verticalization instinct (OpenAI splits FDE by industry; Ajolote has 4 verticals).
  - **Avoid:** Their light/minimal visual language; their lack of a clear *operate/maintain forever* claim (Ajolote's differentiator is permanence — don't let the site read as a one-time deployment shop the way OpenAI's FDE framing can). Avoid leaning on scale/brand as proof — Ajolote can't match $4B, so proof must come from anonymized outcomes.

## Anthropic

- **Positioning:** FDE lives inside Anthropic's **Applied AI** team. Category claim (role-level): engineers who "embed directly with our most strategic customers to drive transformational AI adoption." Note the explicitly MCP-native deliverables — directly aligned to Ajolote's "MCP substrate" thesis.

- **Hero / key language (verbatim from JD):**
  - "As a member of the Applied AI team at Anthropic, you will be a Forward Deployed Engineer (FDE) who embeds directly with our most strategic customers to drive transformational AI adoption."
  - "You will collaborate closely with customer teams to ship advanced AI applications that solve real world business problems."
  - "Deliver technical artifacts for customers like MCP servers, sub-agents, and agent skills that will be used in production workflows."
  - "Provide white glove deployment support for Anthropic products in enterprise environments."
  - "Build long term relationships with customers and proactively identify new opportunities for AI deployment throughout the lifecycle of an engagement."
  - Responsibilities also include: "Identifying and codifying repeatable deployment patterns."

- **IA (section order):** N/A — single greenhouse JD (About the role → responsibilities → qualifications). No marketing IA.

- **Proof pattern:** None on the JD itself — proof is the Anthropic/Claude brand. Industry context (secondary): FDE job postings reportedly grew 800%+ Jan–Sep 2025; Anthropic is scaling the Applied AI group.

- **Engagement framing:** Embed with strategic customers → build production apps on Claude → ship MCP servers / sub-agents / skills → **"long term relationships... throughout the lifecycle of an engagement."** This is the closest of the two labs to Ajolote's operate-and-maintain stake, and it's explicitly **MCP-and-sub-agent-centric**. Travel 25–50%.

- **Visual/motion:** Anthropic house style — warm, editorial, off-white/cream, serif accents. Not Ajolote's direction, but the "lifecycle / long-term relationship" tone is on-thesis.

- **CTA:** "Apply" (recruiting). No consumer CTA.

- **Steal / Avoid for Ajolote:**
  - **Steal:** The MCP-native deliverable vocabulary — "MCP servers, sub-agents, and agent skills... used in production workflows" — is almost verbatim Ajolote's 3-layer engagement (Internal Node / Agents / Outward MCP). Steal "white glove deployment support" and especially **"throughout the lifecycle of an engagement"** — that lifecycle/permanence language is the single best phrase for Ajolote's operator-not-consultancy stake. Steal "codifying repeatable deployment patterns" (= the compounding library).
  - **Avoid:** It's a recruiting artifact, not customer-facing copy — don't lift its structure. Avoid the implication that FDE is a *staffing* function (engineers you hire); Ajolote sells the *outcome+operation*, not bodies. Avoid Anthropic's warm/cream visual register.

## Cross-cutting takeaways for Ajolote

1. **The category language is now blessed by the labs.** "Forward deployed," "embed," "deployment company," "from experimentation to production," "lifecycle of an engagement" — Ajolote can use this vocabulary and inherit credibility instead of inventing terms a non-technical buyer won't parse.
2. **Both labs frame FDE as deploy-into-production; only Anthropic explicitly says long-term/lifecycle.** Ajolote's wedge is to push *past* both: not "we deploy," but "we deploy AND operate it forever." Make permanence the headline differentiator the labs leave on the table.
3. **MCP-native is on-trend, not fringe.** Anthropic literally ships "MCP servers" as the FDE deliverable. Ajolote's MCP-substrate thesis is validated — surface it confidently.
4. **Neither is a visual reference.** Both are light/minimal/editorial. Ajolote keeps its dark cinematic direction; borrow words, not pixels.
