# Cresta

URL(s) read:
- https://cresta.com/
- https://cresta.com/ai-platform (via search)

> **Why it fits:** Cresta sells a **unified platform for human + AI agents** that it deploys *into existing enterprise contact-center stacks* and runs continuously — and its differentiator, "Automation Discovery," is essentially a productized *discovery-then-deploy* engagement (find where to deploy agents, score readiness, project ROI, then operate). That discovery → deploy → run-and-measure arc, plus heavy integration-into-existing-stack framing, is a close read on Ajolote's embed-build-operate motion for an operations-heavy buyer. Vertical: contact center / CX, Fortune-500 scale.

- **Positioning:** "The only unified platform for human and AI Agents built for enterprise scale." Outcome claim: "Save money, increase revenue, and build customer loyalty." Operator framing: deploy multilingual AI "across the entire customer journey—on one secure, scalable platform trusted by the Fortune 500."

- **Hero (verbatim):**
  - Headline: "Transform every customer experience with AI agents"
  - Subhead: "Save money, increase revenue, and build customer loyalty with the only unified platform for human and AI Agents built for enterprise scale."

CTA.

Continuous operation (always-on, human+AI together) rather than a one-time project. Discovery → deploy → operate-and-measure.

This is the single most transferable idea here. Steal **"unified platform for human and AI"** — the human+AI-together framing reassures ops buyers who fear replacement, useful for Ajolote's "AI agents on your existing stack" pitch. Steal the **"integrate into any environment"** + "shared memory... never lost across handoffs" language — maps to Ajolote's Internal Node / unified data brain. Steal the **video-background hero** as a motion reference (fits Ajolote's cinematic dark direction). Steal the **outcome-first headline verbs** (save money / increase revenue / build loyalty) for a non-technical owner.
