# Anduril — cinematic operator hero, scroll narrative

Sources: homepage JS-rendered (fetch returned title only); product pages 403'd. Mechanics combine confirmed design-philosophy reporting + [unverified] layout inferences. Cite: [anduril.com](https://www.anduril.com/), [Fast Company design-lab piece](https://www.fastcompany.com/91527326/anduril-redesigning-future-of-warfare), [Lauren Park (Anduril product designer) portfolio](https://www.laurenjpark.com/work/anduril).

- **Hero:** Cinematic full-bleed. Design team uses **only practical photography and video** of real systems "in action" with operator storytelling (the "Anduril Cinematic Universe") [Fast Company / WebSearch]. Hero = full-screen footage/photo of hardware in the field, short hard headline overlaid [unverified exact treatment].
- **Section architecture & scroll:** **Linear scroll narrative** — distinct stacked chapters revealed via progressive disclosure (concept → drill into features), each chapter a full-width visual stage [confirmed pattern on Park portfolio of same team]. Story unfolds as you descend.
- **Proof:** Credibility from realism — real hardware, real operators, sanitized UI flows with labeled interface areas; not logo walls or metric grids.
- **Navigation:** Minimal top nav, dark.
- **Typography & density:** Sans-serif, large heading scales for section breaks, bolded key terms; sparse-editorial density — image-led, text-light.
- **Motion:** Cinematic video does the heavy lifting; UI motion restrained. Reveal-on-scroll for chapter content.
- **Steal:** Operator-POV narrative arc + full-bleed dark chapters that descend like a story.
- **Won't fit:** Real footage of real systems — Ajolote has no shippable client footage and anonymizes cases. Replace with abstract terminal/telemetry motion + anonymized "operator" framing.
