# v5 Redesign — Goals

## 1. What this site is for (primary objective)

**Twin goals, equally weighted (confirmed by Pablo):**
1. **Establish the category** — make "agent infrastructure operator" legible and credible to a buyer who has never heard the term.
2. **Convert** — drive qualified discovery calls (Calendly).

These are co-equal: the IA carries an educational narrative (Palantir/Distyl-style gravitas) AND every section ends in an ask. The site is not the sale — it makes the category legible *and* earns the call.

## 2. The category we have to establish

Per `00-thesis.md`: Ajolote is **not** a SaaS vendor, **not** a consultancy, **not** a system integrator, **not** a model lab. It is the **operator** of the agent substrate — "build **and maintain**" is the business model.

The site's hardest job: say that in language a non-technical owner understands, without sounding like a dev shop. The differentiator to land: **we don't ship and leave — we operate it, and you own it.**

## 3. Audience (in priority order)

1. **Primary — the economic buyer.** Owner / COO / GM of a 50–500-person, ops-heavy company in Mexico. Verticals (from beachhead): CPG/spirits, healthcare/clinics, loyalty & B2B2C, industrial SME. Non-technical. Cares about: money lost to manual work, whether this is real, whether they're locked in, who's accountable when it breaks.
   - **Addressing decision (Pablo, confirmed): VERTICAL-AGNOSTIC.** Speak to "ops-heavy companies" broadly; let the anonymized cases imply the verticals. No single-vertical hero. (Distyl/Palantir breadth model.)
2. **Secondary — the technical evaluator.** Will dig into proof and architecture. Needs enough signal that this is real engineering, not slideware.
3. **Tertiary — talent.** FDE-track engineers reading the site as a "do I want to work here" signal.

## 3b. Core message / hero anchor (Pablo, confirmed)

**Lead with the BRAIN / unification** — e.g. "One brain over every tool you already run." The hero leads with the *what* (concrete, visual), then the operator verb ("we build it, we run it, we never leave") and the money/pain follow immediately beneath. This anchor doubles as the central visual metaphor for motion (the brain unifying a scattered stack).

- Rejected as *lead* (still used lower): operator-verb-first, pain/money-first.

## 4. The one action

**Book a discovery call** → `calendly.com/hello-ajolotelabs`. One conversion ask per section. No competing CTAs.

## 5. Success metrics

- Discovery calls booked (primary)
- Qualified-lead rate of those calls
- Scroll-depth reaching Proof / Cases
- (Soft) category legibility — can a cold visitor restate what we do in one sentence?

## 6. Non-goals (what this site is NOT)

- Not a self-serve SaaS signup or pricing-tier page
- Not a blog / thought-leadership hub
- No founder profile, no About-Pablo, no team page (permanent positioning stake)
- Not a feature list — we sell an operating model, not modules

## 7. Constraints / stakes (carry into every variant)

- **Dark theme only** — cyan-tinted dark `#0a1a24` base. Never white cards/backgrounds.
- **Anonymized case studies** — no client names on the public site.
- **No geo markers** (no "Mexico City / Austin, TX").
- Satoshi (display) + General Sans (body) + JetBrains Mono (mono).
- All CTAs → Calendly.
- **Lighthouse discipline** — current prod holds 100/100/100/100. Any motion/library (e.g. anime.js) must not regress below the agreed bar.
