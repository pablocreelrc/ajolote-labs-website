# Cases by vertical — research + reframed copy

How leading AI-agent firms present customer stories **by industry/vertical** (not by company), and how to re-present Ajolote's two cases that way. Companion to `competitor-research.md` and `content-v2.md`. Research date: 2026-06-27.

**Hard stops honored:** no client logos, no invented numbers. The only real figure in scope is Mezcal's **"80% automated company" — a TARGET, labeled as such**, never an achieved metric. Desclub stays fully qualitative.

---

## §1 — The by-vertical presentation pattern (distilled)

### 1a. The nav has two axes: FUNCTION × INDUSTRY
Every firm slices use cases on two axes and picks which one leads:
- **Function-led** (then industry as a secondary cut): **HappyRobot** (Customer Support / Sales / Finance / Operations / HR, *then* Logistics / Utilities / Airlines / Retail / Manufacturing / Insurance) and **Cresta** (Sales / Service / Care / Collections / Retention, industry named only as "fit").
- **Industry-led** (vertical is the primary cut): **Sierra** (7 verticals: Financial Services, Healthcare, Telecom, Media, Travel-Transport-Hospitality, Retail & Consumer Goods, Technology) and **Decagon** (`/industry/financial-services`, plus Tech, Travel, Health pages).

Takeaway for Ajolote: with only **two** cases, an industry-led cut reads cleaner than a function grid — it lets each case *own* a vertical instead of competing inside a matrix. Sierra/Decagon is the closer model than HappyRobot's function grid.

Sources: HappyRobot home/nav <https://www.happyrobot.ai/> · Sierra <https://sierra.ai/industries> · Decagon <https://decagon.ai/industry/financial-services>, <https://decagon.ai/case-studies> · Cresta <https://cresta.com/solutions/use-cases/>

### 1b. The vertical card / story structure (consistent across all four)
A vertical card or story page runs the same four beats, in order:

1. **Vertical label** — the industry, not the company (company name, if shown, is secondary/inside).
2. **Problem framed in the buyer's words** — a customer-challenge line *before* any solution. Sierra leads each card with a challenge ("Build stronger client relationships," "Provide always-on, personalized care"). HappyRobot frames the mess: phone-heavy ops, "routine data exchange," "peak volumes overwhelmed teams," "manual fraud screening can't keep pace."
3. **What the agents do** — grouped task list scoped to that vertical (status checks, carrier comms, exception escalation, invoice/rate validation…).
4. **Proof / outcome** — see two schools below.

HappyRobot's full story layout (Kuehne+Nagel): headline (vertical + partnership) → hero → **4-KPI metric dashboard** → "products used" → exec quote → about the partnership → pilot detail → expansion roadmap → CEO quote → CTA.
Source: <https://www.happyrobot.ai/customer-story/kuehne-nagel>

### 1c. Two proof schools (pick one per case)
- **School A — hard-metric dashboard.** HappyRobot and Decagon. Autonomy %, task counts, ROI:
  - Kuehne+Nagel: *10,000+ status checks, 6,000+ emails, 78% of connected calls handled end-to-end, 47% capacity increase.* (<https://www.happyrobot.ai/customer-story/kuehne-nagel>)
  - Circle Logistics: *18% of all freight booked zero-touch, 80–100% reduction in manual calls, 10% higher margins, 100% of calls answered 24/7, 5x+ ROI.* (<https://www.happyrobot.ai/blog/circle-logistics-x-happyrobot-case-study>)
  - WWEX: *6 agents alongside ~120 reps, ~50,000 tasks in the first full month.* (search) (<https://www.upstartsmedia.com/p/happyrobot-spanish-founders-ai-logistics>)
  - Decagon: *Chime 70% AI resolution + doubled NPS; Bilt $1.75M cost reduction, 75% resolution; Substack 90% resolution.* (<https://decagon.ai/case-studies>)
- **School B — testimonial / relationship.** Sierra. **No metrics** — company name + exec quote, framed as "AI frees the team for the human work." ("Frees up our team to focus on community-building." — AG1.) (<https://sierra.ai/industries>)

Ajolote's cases are currently qualitative → we are in **School B today**, with the *option* to add one School-A number per case (Mezcal target only). See §4.

### 1d. The operator voice to borrow (and where HappyRobot can't follow us)
Phrases that carry the operator register, verbatim:
- "Intelligence that runs your operations." / "Put agents to work in complex environments." / "Deployed in the real world."
- The mess: *"fragmented, operationally messy, exception-ridden"* operations; *"the phone needed to become a relationship tool, not a data exchange mechanism"*; *"focus on the 99% of common interactions rather than chasing edge cases."*
- Pillars: **Custom / Complex / Multi-function**; **Agents / Platform / Context** ("The limit of what AI can do is not the model. It's what the model knows.").

**Whitespace none of them claim** (our wedge, keep it on every card): **you own it / no lock-in**, **governance + audit as the product**, **co-build with your experts**, **we stay and operate it**. HappyRobot/Sierra/Decagon are all vendor-run black boxes you rent.

## §2 — Recommended vertical taxonomy for our two cases

**Recommendation: surface concrete, buyer-recognizable INDUSTRY labels — not the internal canonical taxonomy.**

The vision stack's canonical 4 verticals (Finance / Agriculture / Healthcare / **Industrial-SME**) are an *internal* moat/portfolio map. On the site, the by-vertical pattern works only if a buyer self-identifies with the label — "Industrial / SME ops" is an internal bucket, not how a spirits exporter or a loyalty operator describes itself. Sierra and Decagon both use industry names buyers say about themselves ("Financial Services," "Retail & Consumer Goods"), never an internal portfolio code.

| Case | Surfaced vertical (visible) | Maps to canonical (internal) |
|---|---|---|
| Mezcal | **Consumer Goods & Cross-Border Trade** | Industrial / SME ops |
| Desclub | **Consumer Loyalty & Retention** | (cross-cutting; data/agents layer) |

Why these two labels:
- **"Consumer Goods & Cross-Border Trade"** — names both the product category (CPG/spirits) and the hard part (cross-border distribution, customs, SAT, margin/pricing across systems). More legible and more differentiated than "Trade & Distribution → Cross-Border Spirits" (current), and it scales to the next CPG/SME client without a relabel.
- **"Consumer Loyalty & Retention"** — leads with the industry (loyalty), not the deal type ("Enterprise B2B → Loyalty Platform," current, which describes *us* selling, not *their* world). Pairs cleanly with the turnaround story.

Keep the two-line treatment Sierra uses: **vertical label** (big) + a one-line **challenge** under it. Don't build a function grid for two cases.

## §3 — Proposed reframed copy (per case)

Format per card: **VERTICAL LABEL · one complex-problem line · what-we-built line · outcome line.** Two variants each. All qualitative (no numbers); §4 covers the optional single number.

### CASE 01 — Mezcal  ·  vertical: **Consumer Goods & Cross-Border Trade**

**Variant A — leak-led (matches current "margin leaks" anchor):**
- **Vertical:** Consumer Goods & Cross-Border Trade
- **Problem:** "A spirits brand's margin leaks where the systems stop talking — pricing, customs, tax, and distribution, each in its own silo."
- **What we built:** "One brain over the whole cross-border supply chain, with AI employees that reconcile pricing and tax across every system and flag margin leaks *before* a shipment leaves."
- **Outcome:** "Leaks caught before product ships — not found in the quarter-end review."

**Variant B — autonomy-led (sets up the §4 target number):**
- **Vertical:** Consumer Goods & Cross-Border Trade
- **Problem:** "Running a cross-border spirits operation by hand — SAT filings, cash-flow, pricing across borders — is a full-time job that scales with every case shipped."
- **What we built:** "The company brain plus AI employees that map the supply chain end to end: SAT integration, cash-flow prediction, and margin-leak detection across tools that never connected."
- **Outcome:** "The manual operation, automated piece by piece — on the path to a near-fully-automated company." *(see §4 for the target metric)*

### CASE 02 — Desclub  ·  vertical: **Consumer Loyalty & Retention**

**Variant A — turnaround-led (matches current anchor):**
- **Vertical:** Consumer Loyalty & Retention
- **Problem:** "A loyalty platform carrying years of manual workflows — every one a place revenue and time leak out."
- **What we built:** "An AI-native turnaround: a company brain where AI employees find the next manual workflow, automate it, and move to the next — plus an MCP layer that ships intelligence straight into Desclub's consumer products."
- **Outcome:** "A turnaround that compounds — the operation gets leaner every month, not just once."

**Variant B — distribution-led (foregrounds the MCP wedge):**
- **Vertical:** Consumer Loyalty & Retention
- **Problem:** "Loyalty lives or dies on the data layer — and theirs was trapped in manual ops, invisible to the products that needed it."
- **What we built:** "We rebuilt the operation around one brain, automated the manual work month over month, and shipped an MCP distribution layer that puts that intelligence inside their consumer products."
- **Outcome:** "Manual work compounds *down*; product intelligence compounds *up* — month over month."

> Cross-card breadth line (Sierra-style, keep): *"In production across consumer goods, cross-border trade, and consumer loyalty operations."*
> Per-card wedge tag (our whitespace, one line under each outcome): *"You own the brain — the code and the data stay yours."*

## §4 — Proof options per case

| Case | Fully-qualitative (School B, safe today) | +1 defensible number (School A) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mezcal** | Outcome line as written ("leaks caught before product ships"). | The **"80% automated company" TARGET** — *only* if labeled a target/goal, never an achievement. Copy: **"Target: an 80%-automated company."** or a stage pill `GOAL · 80% AUTOMATED`. Use Variant B, which is built to land it. | **Allowed, with the target framing.** This is the one number on the page that isn't qualitative — its honesty (a labeled goal) is itself on-brand for the governance/audit wedge. Do **not** phrase it as "we automated 80%." |
| **Desclub** | Outcome line as written ("compounds month over month"). | **None available** — no defensible real metric, and inventing one is a hard-stop violation. The "compounds" framing *is* the proof (a direction, not a figure). | **Stay fully qualitative.** Resist symmetry pressure to put a number here just because Mezcal has one. |

Asymmetry is fine and honest: Mezcal carries one labeled **target**; Desclub carries a **trajectory** ("compounds"). That mirrors School B (Sierra ships zero metrics and still reads credible) while taking the one defensible number we have. Real achieved metrics slot in pre-launch when defensible (already flagged "Open" in `content-v2.md`).
